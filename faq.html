FAQ

How do I start a career in technology?

Begin by exploring different tech roles, developing foundational skills through online courses, coding bootcamps, and personal projects. Network with professionals and consider internships or entry-level positions.

What skills are most important for tech careers?

Key skills include programming languages (Python, JavaScript), problem-solving, critical thinking, continuous learning, communication, and adaptability. Soft skills are just as crucial as technical skills.

Do I need a university degree to work in tech?

While degrees can be helpful, many tech roles value skills and experience over formal education. Certifications, bootcamps, online courses, and demonstrable projects can also open doors.

How can I build a strong tech portfolio?

Create personal projects, contribute to open-source initiatives, participate in hackathons, showcase your work on platforms like GitHub, and document your learning journey.

What are the highest-paying tech roles?

Roles like Software Engineer, Cloud Architect, Cybersecurity Specialist, Data Scientist, and AI/Machine Learning Engineer often command high salaries. Continuous skill development is key.

How do I know a career in technology is for me?

If you have always been interested in technology, problem solving, wanting to know how things work, or just computers in general a career in tech might be for you. To see what sector you particularly enjoy try find a open day or event to attend by using our helpful links our home page.

Do I need to know how to program or have a computer related qualification to pursue an apprenticeship in tech?

No often employees will be open to people with a range of skill sets as a lot of computing is related your problem solving abilities and your creative thinking. So feel free to check out some websites to see what these companies are asking for.
